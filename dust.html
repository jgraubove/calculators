ΔE > 1.5 Surface: 0%

Visualise how dust accumulation will change the appearance of a 3D object. Dust becomes theoretically visible when the color change is ΔE > 1.5. However, at this level, visibility needs excellent lighting and a clear contrast with a clean area (for example, a smudge).

Research shows that most observers find a surface visibly dusty above 5% area coverage. The ongoing research of Xilan Wu at UCL explores whether this perceptual threshold depends on texture, material and context of exhibition.
