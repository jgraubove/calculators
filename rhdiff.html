About this tool

At first sight, the question seems simple: Are these two RH measurements different?

In practice, this can mean two different things.

1. Is there evidence that the environments are genuinely different?

This is a statistical question. The tool uses Welch's t-test to assess whether the observed difference could plausibly arise from normal variation in the measurements.

2. Is the difference large enough to matter?

This is a practical question. Even if a difference is statistically detectable, it may be small compared with measurement uncertainty and therefore of limited importance for conservation decisions.

Why show both?

A difference can be statistically significant but too small to be practically important. Equally, a potentially important difference may not reach statistical significance if only a small amount of data is available.

For this reason, the tool reports both:

  • Practical significance — is the difference large relative to uncertainty?
  • Statistical evidence — is there evidence that the difference is real?

The overall interpretation combines these two perspectives to support conservation decision-making.
